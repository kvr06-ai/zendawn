Experience East India

From the misty tea gardens of Darjeeling to the cultural richness of Bengal, discover the unspoiled beauty of Eastern India.

The Unexplored Eastern Frontier

A blend of mountain vistas, verdant tea estates, and rich cultural heritage

East India offers travelers a unique glimpse into a less-explored side of the subcontinent. The region encompasses West Bengal, with its cultural capital Kolkata, the tea estates of Darjeeling and Assam, and the pristine landscapes of the Northeast states.

The eastern Himalayan foothills provide a backdrop for some of India's most spectacular scenery, while the Brahmaputra and Ganges rivers shape both the landscape and the cultural traditions of the communities that live along their banks.

The cultural fabric of East India is woven with influences from Tibet, Bhutan, Myanmar, and Bangladesh, creating a rich tapestry of traditions, cuisines, and artistic expressions that differ distinctly from other parts of India.

Our East India guides emphasize mindful exploration of this diverse region, focusing on community-based tourism, cultural preservation, and sustainable practices that help protect the fragile ecosystems of the Eastern Himalayas.

Kaziranga: Realm of the One-Horned Rhino
